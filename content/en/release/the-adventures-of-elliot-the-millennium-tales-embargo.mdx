Combat is built around a dynamic dual-weapon system. Elliot can equip up to two weapons simultaneously and swap between them in real time. This mechanic encourages players to pair a reliable close-range weapon with a specialized tool for ranged pressure, crowd control, or environmental puzzle-solving.

### Magicite Build Customization

Magicite fragments are slotted directly into weapons to alter their behaviors, scale raw statistics, or add passive combat effects. Players can collect fragments by defeating enemies and exploring hidden dungeons across different eras. Following player feedback from the initial debut demo, the developers refined the Magicite interface, adding dedicated weapon-specific Magicite Boxes, improved sorting filters, and an automatic duplicate-dismantling system in the final game.

<Callout type="tip" title="Build Suggestion">
For an optimal early-game build, pair the **Sword and Shield** with the **Bow**. Slot guard-focused Magicite into the shield to maximize stamina recovery, and use the Bow's multi-arrow special attack to weaken groups of enemies before engaging in close combat.
</Callout>

### Local Co-op Implementation

The local 1-2 player support allows a second player to drop in and take full control of Faie. This changes the dynamic of dungeon exploration, as the second player can scout ahead, distract enemies, and coordinate spell combos while the first player focuses on melee combat.

<Callout type="info" title="Revival Mechanic">
In both solo and co-op play, if Elliot falls in battle, Faie can revive him instantly by spending a set amount of **tul** (the game's primary currency), allowing players to continue tough boss encounters without restarting from a checkpoint.
</Callout>

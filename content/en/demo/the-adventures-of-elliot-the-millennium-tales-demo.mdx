<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Faie Magic and Co-op Mechanics">

Faie is not merely a narrative companion; she is a fully active participant in both combat and puzzle-solving. By default, solo players can control Faie independently using the right analog stick. Alternatively, the game supports local 1-2 player co-op, allowing a second player to take direct control of Faie using a second controller.

Faie's capabilities expand significantly as she discovers Shrines of the Mystic throughout the world. Each shrine unlocks a unique support spell that changes how you interact with the environment and enemies.

| Magic Ability | Utility Category | Primary Puzzle Use | Primary Combat Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Warp** | Movement | Crossing gaps and hazards | Repositioning behind enemies |
| **Ignite** | Offense / Interaction | Lighting torches, melting ice | Detonating active bombs |
| **Vacuum** | Control / Gathering | Pulling distant objects | Grouping enemies for burst |
| **Copy** | Coordination | Activating dual switches | Mirroring projectile setups |

Using these spells in tandem with Elliot's physical attacks is the key to mastering the game's combat flow. For example, you can throw a Bomb near a group of shielded enemies, use Faie's Vacuum magic to pull them directly onto the explosive, and then immediately cast Ignite to detonate the bomb instantly for massive damage.

<Callout type="success" title="Co-op Mode Advantage">
When playing in local co-op, the second player controlling Faie can scout dangerous rooms ahead of Elliot. Because Faie cannot take direct damage from standard physical traps, she can safely trigger pressure plates and disarm hazards before Elliot enters.
</Callout>

</Section>
